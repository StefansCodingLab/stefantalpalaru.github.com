Bachelor Thesis: Advanced numerical and experimental analysis of motorcycle crash

In this thesis, I investigated the sliding distances of motorcycles, mopeds, and bicycles using analytical, numerical, and experimental methods. The goal was to understand the dynamics influencing these distances and to contribute to improvements in traffic safety and vehicle design.

Research Overview

Sliding distance—the distance a vehicle travels after a sudden stop—is a critical factor in traffic safety. This research aimed to compare the sliding distances of motorcycles, mopeds, and bicycles to identify the key factors influencing these distances.

Methodology

  • Analytical Models:
    • Developed using Newtonian mechanics.
    • Incorporated variables such as vehicle mass, friction coefficient, and initial speed.
  • Numerical Simulations:
    • Utilized computational tools like MATLAB.
    • Conducted sensitivity analyses and validated models against analytical predictions.
  • Experimental Analysis:
    • Conducted controlled experiments on various surfaces (e.g., asphalt, gravel).
    • Measured sliding distances with high-speed cameras and sensors.
❮ ❯

Findings

  • Analytical Findings: Equations demonstrated the significant impact of initial speed and friction coefficients on sliding distances.
  • Numerical Simulations: Confirmed the reliability of the analytical models and provided consistent results across various conditions.
  • Experimental Data: Revealed significant differences in sliding distances among vehicle types:
    • Motorcycles exhibited the longest sliding distances due to higher mass and speed potential.
    • Mopeds and bicycles showed shorter sliding distances, with bicycles stopping the quickest.
  • Key Factors Identified: Friction coefficients and initial speeds.

Conclusion

This study offers a comprehensive analysis of the sliding distances of motorcycles, mopeds, and bicycles, providing valuable insights into vehicle dynamics during sudden stops. The findings contribute to traffic safety and can inform better vehicle design.

Future Research Recommendations

  • Explore additional factors such as rider posture, tire properties, and environmental conditions.
  • Expand the experimental dataset to further validate and refine the findings.
